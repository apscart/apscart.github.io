Fred P. Schoville


Fred P. Schoville serves as Vice President of Technology Development addressing the technological manufacturing and design needs for the Company, specifically the design and development of prototypes, software models as well as troubleshooting new products and technology. Mr. Schoville has extensive expertise in manufacturing and design and has developed the physical components of the MDC since its infancy. Prior to his involvement with APS, Mr. Schoville served as Vice President of Operations of Galaxy Machine and Retrofit ("GMR") in Detroit, Michigan. During his tenure at GMR, Mr. Schoville was responsible for the design, development and manufacturing of both domestic and international projects whereby he interacted with a network of engineers in the U.S., Europe, China, India, and Mexico. As an innovator in the automotive supply industry, Mr.Schoville has extensive experience in supply chain automation. In 2007 he was selected by the State of Michigan to assess, evaluate and develop other areas of manufacturing outside of the legacy automotive industry. Similarities in the automotive manufacturing technology led Mr. Schoville to the healthcare industry, specifically to the development of medical delivery systems. Mr. Schoville's manufacturing background includes technical and administrative positions in a variety of settings including an apprenticeship and senior managerial positions in the controls engineering department at Ex-Cell-O Corporation located in Rockford, Illinois. His engineering experience includes positions at Rockwell Corporation's Rocky Flats Nuclear Weapons Production Facility located near Denver, Colorado, with emphasis on CNC control systems researching nuclear weapons systems. Mr. Schoville's extensive engineering experience includes proficiency with CNC machinery, robotics, lasers and plastics. Mr. Schoville received a bachelor's in Electrical Engineering and Technology from Lawrence Technological University in 1993 and his MBA from University of Michigan in 2006.
